Work Signals

Releasing November 2026

About the Book

Why do smart, hardworking people get overlooked at work?

Why do their ideas go unheard, their work unnoticed, and their names left out of the conversations that shape what happens next?

Because careers do not take off with talent alone. They take off when you understand how value gets recognized. Most of us are taught how to work hard, but almost no one is taught how to play the game of work.

What if work came with a playbook? A way to learn the unwritten rules, read the dynamics beneath the surface, and understand how decisions are really made?

Work Signals gives you a practical guide to win at work. It shows you how to create value that matters, make your contributions visible without becoming performative or manipulative, build trust that compounds, read culture and power dynamics with clarity, create options, and stay adaptable in a world that keeps changing.

About the Authors

In Work Signals, Ruchin and Anthony bring together research depth, operating rigor, and field-tested insight for leaders building organizations where people and performance can thrive. They decode how organizations actually work, what it takes for people to thrive inside them, and how to make your value impossible to overlook.

Ruchin Kansal

Ruchin Kansal advises senior leaders on high-stakes, transformative business decisions.

He brings 25 years of senior experience in architecture, business, and leadership science, which has shaped his systems view of organizations. In corporate, academic, and advisory roles, he decodes how large, complex enterprises work and how people at all levels can act with clarity and leverage.

He has served as Global SVP of digital strategy at Siemens Healthineers, and head of Business Innovation and Transformation at Boehringer Ingelheim. Earlier, he held roles at Deloitte, Capgemini US, and Ernst & Young, helping biopharma and MedTech launch new products, services and expand into new geographies.

Ruchin is Professor of Practice at Seton Hall University and Visiting Professor of Leadership at Brown University. He leads the IRB-approved, five-year Future of Leadership Survey and founded In the Lead, Seton Hall's leadership magazine.

He co-authored Redefining Innovation (Routledge) and The Kansal Clunker (Koehler, 2025). He has contributed to Oxford and World Scientific volumes, published in Wharton Healthcare Quarterly, and writes Level Zero Leadership, a Substack on work dynamics.

He has presented at the White House, SIOP, and the Wharton Global C-Suite Executive Program.

Ruchin holds an MBA from NYU Stern, a Bachelor's in Architecture from the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, and a Master's in Architecture from Kansas State University.

Anthony W. Caputo

Anthony W. Caputo is a systems-oriented organizational psychologist, consultant, and company-building operator who helps leaders turn human behavior into business performance.

As Chief Operating Officer and Chief People Officer at Remesh, he has built and led the systems that connect talent, leadership, and execution to company results, including its transition to a four-day workweek and recognition on Deloitte’s Technology Fast 500.

His work on organizational dynamics spans consulting, research, and executive leadership across high-growth companies, academia, and global institutions. Through work with Stanford Graduate School of Business, Columbia Business School, the United Nations, Mercer, The Conference Board, and the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, he has developed a rare combination of advisory depth, operating judgment, and institutional range.

His scholarship and applied work on employee engagement and retention, leadership effectiveness, and the future of work have been published or presented through Cambridge University Press, Oxford University Press, Wiley, The Conference Board, and the Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology.

He holds degrees from Teachers College, Columbia University, and Seton Hall University.
